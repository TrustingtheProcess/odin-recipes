Grilled Lemon Pepper Chicken

Description

Grilled lemon pepper chicken is a tantalizing dish that marries the smokey char of grilled chicken with the bright, zesty flavors of lemon and the aromatic punch of cracked black pepper. Picture succulent chicken breasts or thighs, marinated in a vibrant blend of freshly squeezed lemon juice, zest, olive oil, garlic, and a generous sprinkling of cracked black pepper, infusing the meat with layers of tangy and spicy goodness. As it grills to perfection, the chicken develops a beautifully caramelized exterior, locking in moisture and creating a tantalizing contrast to the tender, juicy meat inside. The lemon adds a refreshing acidity that cuts through the richness of the chicken, while the pepper lends a subtle heat and earthiness that enhances the overall flavor profile. Served hot off the grill, this dish is a celebration of simplicity and flavor, perfect for a summer barbecue or a cozy weeknight dinner. Whether enjoyed on its own or paired with vibrant sides like roasted vegetables, crisp salads, or fluffy rice, grilled lemon pepper chicken is sure to delight the senses with its irresistible combination of textures and flavors.

Ingredients

  • ¼ cup frershly squeezed lemon juice
  • 3 large cloves garlic, finely grated
  • 2 tablespoons canola oil
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh rosemary
  • 2 ½ teaspoons kosher salt
  • 2 teaspoons freshly ground black pepper
  • 2 teaspoons Dijon mustard
  • 2 (10 ounce) skinless, boneless chjicken breasts
  • lemon wedges for serving

Steps

  1. Gather all ingredients
  2. Stir together lemon juice, garlic, oil, rosemary, salt, pepper, and mustard in a bowl until well combined. Using a fork, prick each chicken breasgt all over 8 to 10 times.
  3. Add chicken breast to bowl with lemon juice mixture and toss to coat completely, using your hands to gently massage lemon juice mixture into each chicken breast. Cover bowl with plastic wrap and refridgerate for 1 hour.
  4. Preheat an outdoor grill for medium-high heat and lightly oil the grate.
  5. Place chicken breasts on oiled grates; grill, covered, until chicken breasts are lightly charred, no longer pink in the center and the juices run clear, 10 to 12 minutes per side. An instant-read thermometer inserted into the center should read 165 degrees F (74 degrees C).
  6. Transfer chicken breasts to serving platter; serve with lemon wedges.
